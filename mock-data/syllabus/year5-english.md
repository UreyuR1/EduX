# Year 5 English — Term 2 Syllabus

**Teacher:** Ms. Sarah Chen
**School:** EduX Primary School
**Curriculum:** Australian Curriculum v9.0 — English

---

## Week 1–2: Narrative Writing — Story Structure (AC9E5LE05)

### Learning Outcomes
- Identify the elements of narrative structure: orientation, complication, climax, resolution
- Plan and draft a short narrative with a clear beginning, middle, and end
- Use descriptive language including similes and metaphors
- Edit and proofread own writing for spelling, punctuation, and grammar

### Key Activities
- Story mountain mapping: breaking down favourite stories into structural elements
- Sensory writing workshop: describing settings using all five senses
- Peer editing circles: giving and receiving constructive feedback

### Assessment
- Narrative draft submission (Week 2, Friday)

---

## Week 3–4: Reading Comprehension — Inference and Prediction (AC9E5LY03)

### Learning Outcomes
- Make inferences based on textual evidence
- Distinguish between literal and inferential comprehension
- Make predictions about texts and verify them while reading
- Identify the main idea and supporting details in a passage

### Key Activities
- "Reading detective" activities: finding clues in texts to answer inferential questions
- Prediction journals: writing predictions before, during, and after reading
- Guided reading groups with levelled texts
- Comprehension strategy posters: visualising, questioning, connecting

### Assessment
- Reading comprehension test (Week 4, Wednesday)

---

## Week 5–6: Persuasive Writing (AC9E5LY06)

### Learning Outcomes
- Understand the purpose and structure of persuasive texts
- Use persuasive devices: rhetorical questions, emotive language, evidence, repetition
- Write a structured persuasive text with a clear position, arguments, and conclusion
- Use linking words and phrases: furthermore, in addition, however, therefore

### Key Activities
- Debate club: informal class debates on age-appropriate topics
- Persuasive letter writing: convincing the principal to change a school rule
- Analysing advertisements: identifying persuasive techniques in real media
- Persuasive vocabulary wall: collecting powerful persuasive words and phrases

### Assessment
- Persuasive essay (Week 6, Friday)
- Homework: write a persuasive letter to a family member

---

## Week 7–8: Grammar and Punctuation Focus (AC9E5LA05)

### Learning Outcomes
- Use complex sentences with subordinating conjunctions (because, although, when, if)
- Apply correct punctuation: commas in lists, apostrophes for possession, direct speech
- Identify and use adverbial phrases to add detail to writing
- Understand subject-verb agreement in complex sentences

### Key Activities
- Sentence combining activities: joining simple sentences into complex ones
- Punctuation hunt: finding and correcting errors in sample texts
- Grammar games: "Conjunction Junction" and "Punctuation Station"

### Assessment
- Grammar and punctuation test (Week 8, Monday)

---

## Week 9–10: Poetry and Creative Expression (AC9E5LE02)

### Learning Outcomes
- Explore different poetry forms: haiku, limerick, free verse, acrostic
- Identify poetic devices: alliteration, onomatopoeia, rhyme, rhythm
- Write original poems using at least two poetic devices
- Perform poetry with expression and confidence

### Key Activities
- Poetry reading circle: sharing and discussing published poems
- Poetry writing workshop: experimenting with different forms
- Class poetry anthology: compiling a collection of student poems
- Poetry slam: performing original poems for the class

### Assessment
- Poetry portfolio submission (Week 10)
- End-of-term English assessment (Week 10, Thursday)
