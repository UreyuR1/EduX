# Year 6 Science — Term 2 Syllabus

**Teacher:** Mr. James Nguyen
**School:** EduX Primary School
**Curriculum:** Australian Curriculum v9.0 — Science

---

## Week 1–2: Earth's Place in Space — The Solar System (AC9S6U02)

### Learning Outcomes
- Describe the structure of the solar system and the relative positions of planets
- Explain why Earth is suitable for supporting life
- Understand the scale of distances in the solar system
- Use scientific vocabulary: orbit, axis, rotation, revolution

### Key Activities
- Scale model of the solar system: using everyday objects to represent planet sizes
- Planet fact file research project: each student researches one planet
- Virtual planetarium visit (online resource)
- Solar system mobile construction

### Assessment
- Planet fact file presentation (Week 2, Thursday)

---

## Week 3–4: Day, Night, and Seasons (AC9S6U02)

### Learning Outcomes
- Explain how Earth's rotation causes day and night
- Explain how Earth's axial tilt and orbit cause seasons
- Understand why different hemispheres experience opposite seasons
- Investigate how day length changes throughout the year

### Key Activities
- Torch and globe demonstration: modelling day/night and seasons
- Shadow investigation: measuring shadow lengths at different times of day
- Seasons comparison project: comparing Australian and Northern Hemisphere seasons
- Data analysis: graphing daylight hours across the year in Melbourne

### Assessment
- Seasons explanation poster (Week 4, Friday)

---

## Week 5–6: Earth's Place in Space — Moon Phases and Eclipses (AC9S6U02)

### Learning Outcomes
- Describe the phases of the Moon and explain why they occur
- Explain the difference between solar and lunar eclipses
- Understand the Moon's effect on tides
- Record and interpret Moon phase observations over time

### Key Activities
- Moon phase observation journal: recording the Moon's appearance each night for two weeks
- Oreo Moon phases: using biscuits to model the eight main Moon phases
- Eclipse simulation: using a torch, tennis ball, and basketball
- Research task: how do tides work?

### Assessment
- Moon phase journal and written explanation (Week 6, Wednesday)
- Homework: complete the Moon observation log

---

## Week 7–8: Biological Sciences — Living Things and Adaptations (AC9S6U01)

### Learning Outcomes
- Describe structural, behavioural, and physiological adaptations of Australian animals
- Explain how adaptations help organisms survive in their environment
- Investigate how environmental changes can affect living things
- Classify living things based on observable features

### Key Activities
- Australian animal adaptation research: focusing on unique fauna (platypus, kangaroo, thorny devil)
- Habitat diorama project: creating a model habitat showing how animals are adapted
- "Survival challenge" game: designing an organism with adaptations for a given environment
- Guest speaker: local wildlife ranger (virtual or in-person)

### Assessment
- Adaptation research report (Week 8, Tuesday)

---

## Week 9–10: Scientific Investigation and Reporting (AC9S6I01)

### Learning Outcomes
- Design a fair test with controlled, independent, and dependent variables
- Collect, record, and analyse data using tables and graphs
- Write a structured scientific report: aim, hypothesis, method, results, conclusion
- Evaluate the reliability of results and suggest improvements

### Key Activities
- Class investigation: "Does the colour of light affect plant growth?"
- Scientific report writing workshop: step-by-step guided report
- Peer review of scientific reports
- Science fair preparation: presenting investigation findings

### Assessment
- Scientific investigation report (Week 10, Monday)
- End-of-term science assessment (Week 10, Friday)
